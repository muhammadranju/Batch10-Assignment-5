Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

The recent Quota Movement, a nationwide protest in Bangladesh demanding a fair quota system for government jobs, has been marked by both peaceful demonstrations and violent clashes. Many individuals, including students, activists, and ordinary citizens, have sustained injuries during these protests. The injuries range from minor bruises to severe wounds, and some individuals have even lost their lives. Providing aid to the injured is a crucial step in supporting the victims of the Quota Movement and ensuring their well-being. Medical assistance, including treatment for injuries, medication, and rehabilitation services, is essential for the recovery of those affected. Additionally, financial assistance can help alleviate the economic burden faced by injured individuals and their families. By providing aid, we can demonstrate solidarity with the victims of the movement and contribute to their physical and emotional healing. Furthermore, supporting the injured can help to raise awareness.

Children in Gaza need life-saving support

The conflict in the Gaza Strip is taking a catastrophic toll on children. More than 14,000 children have reportedly been killed, according to the latest estimate by the Palestinian Ministry of Health; thousands more have been injured. There are no safe spaces. All of Gaza’s children have been exposed to the traumatic experiences of war, the consequences of which will last a lifetime. Around 1.9 million people – about 9 in 10 of Gaza’s population – are estimated to have been internally displaced. Half of them children. They do not have enough access to water, food, fuel and medicine.
